Payload for Ultrahigh Energy Observations (PUEO)

From May to July 2025, I supported final production and payload integration of the Payload for Ultrahigh Energy Observations (PUEO) at NASA's Columbia Scientific Balloon Facility in Palestine, TX. PUEO is a NASA Pioneers mission studying Askaryan emission from ultra-high-energy neutrinos interacting with the Antarctic ice. Working alongside 50+ researchers from 11 universities across the U.S., UK, and Taiwan, I performed subsystem integration, surface preparation, and finishing on 300+ precision components, resolved tolerance nonconformities, conducted part testing, and drafted engineering documentation supporting the integration effort. PUEO launched in December 2025 and flew above Antarctica for over a month of data collection.

Cosmic Ray Lunar Sounder (CoRaLS)

CoRaLS (Cosmic Ray Lunar Sounder) is a spacecraft concept developed to detect water ice buried beneath the lunar surface. When ultra-high-energy cosmic rays strike the Moon, they produce brief radio pulses in the regolith (Askaryan emission); allowing for mapping of ice deposits for targeted for future crewed missions. I fabricated prototypes of the mission's proprietary antenna design. I also contributed to the design of a deployable assembly that stows the antennas compactly within the launch envelope and unfolds them to full aperture on orbit, balancing stowed volume, deployment reliability, and the antenna's RF geometry, which can't be distorted by the supporting structure.

Askaryan Calorimeter Experiment (ACE)

The Askaryan Calorimeter Experiment (ACE) is a Fermilab/UH High Energy Physics Group project studying the Askaryan effect as a compact, inexpensive way to measure particle energies. PLA Infill Characterization Study

For my AME 341b instrumentation course, my team investigated how different 3D printed infill pattern and end conditions affect the buckling behavior of slender PLA columns. We tested 36 FDM-printed specimens across three infill patterns (linear, hexagonal, tri-hexagonal) and three end conditions (fixed-fixed, fixed-pinned, pinned-pinned), comparing measured critical loads and effective moduli against Euler buckling theory. To run the tests, we modified a tensile-testing rig to apply compressive loads. I was in charge of designing the experimental setup to mount the specimens and prevent off-axis bending. I also reduced the stress-strain data in MATLAB, extracting critical buckling loads, elastic moduli, and effective length constants. Length-varying trials matched Euler predictions within 4–8%, while infill comparisons showed no statistically significant strength difference, a finding that highlighted how much FDM manufacturing variability (interlayer adhesion, print defects) dominates over infill geometry at this scale.

Lunar Regolith Exploration Actuator

This project supports RF characterization of lunar regolith samples for future lunar-exploration missions. I designed and fabricated a linear actuator mount assembly used to position samples inside a large resonance chamber (39.5" × 20" × 3.9") that I also fabricated, allowing the sample–RF interaction to be measured at precisely controlled positions. The mounts went through two design iterations: V1 for the Actuonix P8 50 mm stroke actuator and V2 for the 100 mm stroke version, with V2 redesigned to integrate directly onto the resonance chamber. The system uses high-precision stepper-driven linear actuators, giving fine, repeatable position control during measurements.

Thermovacuum Chamber Redesign

I redesigned the door of a thermovacuum chamber used to simulate lunar conditions for sample and component testing for flight conditions, replacing the original with a cast-acrylic design that gives full visibility into the chamber during experiments, including upcoming lunar-sample actuation tests. I designed the door, validated it with FEA, and tested it under vacuum down to 1 × 10⁻⁵ Torr. I machined the clamping mechanism, cast-acrylic window, and supporting hardware in-house; the O-ring groove was the one outsourced feature, as it exceeded our CNC's work envelope.
